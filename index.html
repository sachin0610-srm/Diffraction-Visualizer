📖 Understanding Diffraction

What is Diffraction?

Diffraction is the bending and spreading of waves when they encounter an obstacle or pass through an aperture. It is a fundamental consequence of the wave nature of light. When coherent light passes through a narrow slit, it produces a characteristic pattern of bright and dark fringes on a distant screen.

Single-Slit Diffraction

When light passes through a single slit of width a, each point across the slit opening acts as a source of secondary wavelets (Huygens' principle). These wavelets interfere, producing the intensity pattern:

\[ I(\theta) = I_0 \left[\frac{\sin\beta}{\beta}\right]^2, \quad \beta = \frac{\pi a \sin\theta}{\lambda} \]

Minima occur when \(\sin\theta = m\lambda/a\) for integer \(m \neq 0\). The central maximum contains about 84% of the total intensity.

Multi-Slit Diffraction (Gratings)

With N equally-spaced slits separated by distance d, the single-slit envelope is modulated by an interference factor:

\[ I(\theta) = I_0 \left[\frac{\sin\beta}{\beta}\right]^2 \times \left[\frac{\sin(N\delta/2)}{N\sin(\delta/2)}\right]^2, \quad \delta = \frac{2\pi d \sin\theta}{\lambda} \]

Principal maxima occur when \(d\sin\theta = m\lambda\) (constructive interference from all slits). Between each pair of principal maxima, there are \(N-2\) secondary maxima and \(N-1\) minima from the interference factor alone.

Phasor Diagram

A phasor diagram visualizes how the N individual wave contributions add together. Each phasor has the same amplitude but is rotated by phase angle \(\delta\) relative to the previous one. The resultant amplitude (and thus intensity) depends on how constructively or destructively the phasors combine.

Key Parameters

λ (Wavelength): 380–700 nm covers the visible spectrum, from violet to red.
a (Slit Width): Narrower slits produce wider diffraction patterns.
d (Slit Spacing): Determines the angular spacing of interference fringes.
N (Number of Slits): More slits produce sharper, more defined maxima.
L (Screen Distance): Affects the scale of the pattern on the observation screen.
